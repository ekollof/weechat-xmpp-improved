       Introduction<a class="anchor-link" href="#intro"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h2>
  <p class="" style="">A nickname is a global, memorable (but not necessarily unique) friendly or informal name chosen by the owner of a bare JID &lt;localpart@domain.tld&gt; for the purpose of associating a distinctive mapping between the person's unique JID and non-unique nickname. While nicknames have been a common feature of instant messaging systems for many years, they have not always featured prominently in Jabber/XMPP IM systems (e.g., nicknames were not specified in <span class="ref" style=""><a href="http://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc3921">RFC 3921</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465384672">1</a>] or <span class="ref" style=""><a href="http://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc6121">RFC 6121</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465380256">2</a>]). However, there are several reasons why nicknames are important:</p>
  <ul class="" style="">
    <li class="" style="">Users like them.</li>
    <li class="" style="">They are easier to remember than JIDs.</li>
    <li class="" style="">They can be used to help prevent mimicking of JIDs (see <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0165.html">Best Practices to Prevent JID Mimicking (XEP-0165)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465372272">3</a>]).</li>
  </ul>
  <p class="" style="">This document defines best practices that enable IM users to advertise their preferred nicknames over Jabber/XMPP instant messaging networks.</p>
<h2 id="terms">2.
       Terminology<a class="anchor-link" href="#terms"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h2>
  <p class="" style="">This proposal draws a distinction between the following kinds of names, where a JID is an innate feature of a user's identity on an XMPP system, a nickname is asserted by a user, and a handle is assigned by a contact to a user.</p>
  <figure class="table"><figcaption id="table-1"><strong>Table 1:</strong> JIDs, Nicknames, and Handles<a class="anchor-link" href="#table-1"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><table>
    <tr class="body">
      <th colspan="" rowspan="">Name</th>
      <th colspan="" rowspan="">Definition</th>
    </tr>
    <tr class="body">
      <td align="" colspan="" rowspan="">Jabber ID (JID)</td>
      <td align="" colspan="" rowspan="">A global and unique XMPP identifier registered to a particular user, of the form &lt;localpart@domain.tld&gt;; represented in the 'from' attribute of XML stanzas sent by that user, the 'jid' attribute of items associated with that user in a contact's roster, etc.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr class="body">
      <td align="" colspan="" rowspan="">Nickname</td>
      <td align="" colspan="" rowspan="">A global and memorable (but not necessarily unique) friendly name or informal name asserted by an IM user. Typically, a nickname is different from a familiar name, such as "Chuck" for "Charles", "Bill" for "William", "Pete" for "Peter", or "Dave" for "David"; instead, a nickname is even less formal, such as "stpeter" or "dizzyd". A nickname is thus typically different from a "display name" as that term is understood in SMTP (see <span class="ref" style=""><a href="http://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc2821">RFC 2821</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465355744">4</a>]) and SIP (see <span class="ref" style=""><a href="http://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc3261">RFC 3261</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465346704">5</a>]).</td>
    </tr>
    <tr class="body">
      <td align="" colspan="" rowspan="">Handle</td>
      <td align="" colspan="" rowspan="">A private, unique, and memorable "petname" or "alias" assigned by a contact to a user; represented in the 'name' attribute of the item associated with that user's JID in the contact's roster.  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465349680">6</a>]</td>
    </tr>
  </table></figure>
<h2 id="format">3.
       Format<a class="anchor-link" href="#format"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h2>
  <p class="" style="">A nickname MUST be encapsulated as the XML character data of a &lt;nick/&gt; element qualified by the 'http://jabber.org/protocol/nick' namespace. Here is an example:</p>
  <figure class="code-example" id="example-1"><figcaption><strong>Example 1.</strong> A Nickname<a class="anchor-link" href="#example-1"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><pre class="prettyprint">
&lt;nick xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/nick'&gt;Ishmael&lt;/nick&gt;
</pre></figure>
  <p class="" style="">A nickname of this form has the same semantic meaning as the following data fields:</p>
  <ul class="" style="">
    <li class="" style="">The "NICKNAME" field specified in <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0054.html">vcard-temp (XEP-0054)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465337584">7</a>].</li>
    <li class="" style="">The "nickname" field specified in <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0154.html">User Profile (XEP-0154)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465327424">8</a>].</li>
    <li class="" style="">The "nickname" field specified in <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0077.html">In-Band Registration (XEP-0077)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465325520">9</a>].</li>
    <li class="" style="">The "nick" field specified in <span class="ref" style=""><a href="http://xmlns.com/foaf/0.1/">Friend of a Friend (FOAF)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465320208">10</a>].</li>
    <li class="" style="">The "Alias" field specified in the <span class="ref">Extensible Name and Address Language</span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465316800">11</a>] developed by <span class="ref" style=""><a href="http://www.oasis-open.org/">OASIS</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465313776">12</a>].</li>
  </ul>
  <p class="" style="">The entity to which the &lt;nick/&gt; refers is the from address (no matter how encapsulated in XML) of the nearest ancestor element that specifies the sender (which might be a parent or grandparent element, e.g. the 'from' attribute of an &lt;iq/&gt; stanza).</p>
<h2 id="usecases">4.
       Use Cases<a class="anchor-link" href="#usecases"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h2>
  <p class="" style="">In general, a user SHOULD include his or her nickname when establishing initial communication with a contact or group of contacts (i.e., the user has never been in communication with and does not have a prior relationship with the contact or group of contacts). Appropriate use cases therefore include:</p>
  <ul class="" style="">
    <li class="" style="">Presence subscription requests</li>
    <li class="" style="">Message exchange</li>
    <li class="" style="">Multi-user chat</li>
    <li class="" style="">Waiting lists</li>
  </ul>
  <div class="indent"><h3 id="subscription">4.1 Presence Subscription Requests<a class="anchor-link" href="#subscription"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h3>
    <p class="" style="">As defined in <span class="ref" style=""><a href="http://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc6121">RFC 6121</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465380256">2</a>], a presence subscription request contains only the JID of the sender:</p>
    <figure class="code-example" id="example-2"><figcaption><strong>Example 2.</strong> A Basic Subscription Request<a class="anchor-link" href="#example-2"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><pre class="prettyprint">
&lt;presence from='narrator@moby-dick.lit' to='starbuck@moby-dick.lit' type='subscribe'/&gt;
</pre></figure>
    <p class="" style="">Naturally, based on the JID of the sender, it is possible for the client to pull information about the sender from a persistent data store such as an LDAP database, <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0054.html">vcard-temp (XEP-0054)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465337584">7</a>] node, or <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0154.html">User Profile (XEP-0154)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465327424">8</a>] store. However, to speed interactions, this document recommends that when a client sends a subscription request, it SHOULD include the preferred nickname of the sender:</p>
    <figure class="code-example" id="example-3"><figcaption><strong>Example 3.</strong> Including Nickname with Subscription Request<a class="anchor-link" href="#example-3"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><pre class="prettyprint">
&lt;presence from='narrator@moby-dick.lit' to='starbuck@moby-dick.lit' type='subscribe'&gt;
  &lt;nick xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/nick'&gt;Ishmael&lt;/nick&gt;
&lt;/presence&gt;
</pre></figure>
    <p class="" style="">Note: This document recommends sending the nickname only in presence subscription requests; the nickname MUST NOT be included in presence broadcasts (i.e., &lt;presence/&gt; stanzas with no 'type' attribute or of type "unavailable").</p>
  </div>
  <div class="indent"><h3 id="message">4.2 Message Exchange<a class="anchor-link" href="#message"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h3>
    <p class="" style="">When a user begins to chat with a contact but the two parties have no pre-existing relationship or prior communications (e.g., no presence subscription or previous message exchange), the user SHOULD include the nickname with the first message sent to the contact:</p>
    <figure class="code-example" id="example-4"><figcaption><strong>Example 4.</strong> Including Nickname with First Message<a class="anchor-link" href="#example-4"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><pre class="prettyprint">
&lt;message from='narrator@moby-dick.lit/pda' to='starbuck@moby-dick.lit' type='chat'&gt;
  &lt;body&gt;Call me Ishmael&lt;/body&gt;
  &lt;nick xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/nick'&gt;Ishmael&lt;/nick&gt;
&lt;/message&gt;
</pre></figure>
  </div>
  <div class="indent"><h3 id="manage">4.3 Nickname Management<a class="anchor-link" href="#manage"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h3>
    <p class="" style="">In order for a user to modify his or her nickname and notify contacts of that change, it is RECOMMENDED for clients to use <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0163.html">Personal Eventing Protocol (XEP-0163)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465277520">13</a>] (a.k.a. PEP).</p>
    <figure class="code-example" id="example-5"><figcaption><strong>Example 5.</strong> User Publishes Updated Nickname to PEP Node<a class="anchor-link" href="#example-5"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><pre class="prettyprint">
&lt;iq from='narrator@moby-dick.lit/pda'
    type='set'
    id='pub1'&gt;
  &lt;pubsub xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/pubsub'&gt;
    &lt;publish node='http://jabber.org/protocol/nick'&gt;
      &lt;item&gt;
        &lt;nick xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/nick'&gt;CallMeIshmael&lt;/nick&gt;
      &lt;/item&gt;
    &lt;/publish&gt;
  &lt;/pubsub&gt;
&lt;/iq&gt;
</pre></figure>
    <figure class="code-example" id="example-6"><figcaption><strong>Example 6.</strong> PEP Node Generates Notifications<a class="anchor-link" href="#example-6"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><pre class="prettyprint">
&lt;message from='narrator@moby-dick.lit'
         to='starbuck@moby-dick.lit'
         type='headline'
         id='foo'&gt;
  &lt;event xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/pubsub#event'&gt;
    &lt;items node='http://jabber.org/protocol/nick'&gt;
      &lt;item&gt;
        &lt;nick xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/nick'&gt;CallMeIshmael&lt;/nick&gt;
      &lt;/item&gt;
    &lt;/items&gt;
  &lt;/event&gt;
  &lt;addresses xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/address'&gt;
    &lt;address type='replyto' jid='narrator@moby-dick.lit/pda'/&gt;
  &lt;/addresses&gt;
&lt;/message&gt;
.
.
.
</pre></figure>
    <p class="" style="">If a XEP-0163-compliant personal eventing service is not available, a client SHOULD use a standalone <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0060.html">Publish-Subscribe (XEP-0060)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465273504">14</a>] service.</p>
    <figure class="code-example" id="example-7"><figcaption><strong>Example 7.</strong> User Publishes Updated Nickname to PubSub Node<a class="anchor-link" href="#example-7"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><pre class="prettyprint">
&lt;iq from='narrator@moby-dick.lit/pda'
    to='pubsub.mody-dick.lit'
    type='set'
    id='pub2'&gt;
  &lt;pubsub xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/pubsub'&gt;
    &lt;publish node='841f3c8955c4c41a0cf99620d78a33b996659ded'&gt;
      &lt;item&gt;
        &lt;nick xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/nick'&gt;CallMeIshmael&lt;/nick&gt;
      &lt;/item&gt;
    &lt;/publish&gt;
  &lt;/pubsub&gt;
&lt;/iq&gt;
</pre></figure>
    <figure class="code-example" id="example-8"><figcaption><strong>Example 8.</strong> PubSub Node Generates Notifications<a class="anchor-link" href="#example-8"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><pre class="prettyprint">
&lt;message from='pubsub.mody-dick.lit'
         to='starbuck@moby-dick.lit'
         type='headline'
         id='foo'&gt;
  &lt;event xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/pubsub#event'&gt;
    &lt;items node='841f3c8955c4c41a0cf99620d78a33b996659ded'&gt;
      &lt;item&gt;
        &lt;nick xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/nick'&gt;CallMeIshmael&lt;/nick&gt;
      &lt;/item&gt;
    &lt;/items&gt;
  &lt;/event&gt;
  &lt;addresses xmlns='http://jabber.org/protocol/address'&gt;
    &lt;address type='replyto' jid='narrator@moby-dick.lit/pda'/&gt;
  &lt;/addresses&gt;
&lt;/message&gt;
.
.
.
</pre></figure>
    <p class="" style="">If a client does not support <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0060.html">Publish-Subscribe (XEP-0060)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465273504">14</a>] or the subset thereof specified in <span class="ref">XEP-0163</span>, it MAY send one &lt;message/&gt; stanza to each of its contacts, containing the updated nickname (note: the client SHOULD send the messages in a staggered fashion in order to avoid server-enforced rate limiting, commonly known as "karma").</p>
    <figure class="code-example" id="example-9"><figcaption><strong>Example 9.</strong> Nickname Change Notification via Message<a class="anchor-link" href="#example-9"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></figcaption><pre class="prettyprint">

<h2 id="impl">5.
       Implementation Notes<a class="anchor-link" href="#impl"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h2>
  <p class="" style="">An IM client MAY use the user's own nickname as all or part of the "display name" shown to the user of that client (e.g., as the sending name in one-to-one chats and groupchats). For example, if the user whose JID is narrator@moby-dick.lit asserts that his nickname is "Ishmael", that user's client may show "Ishmael" as all or part of the user's display name. How the client shall store the display name is out of scope for this document; possible mechanisms include the user's local vCard, an organizational LDAP directory, <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0049.html">Private XML Storage (XEP-0049)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465258592">15</a>], or <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0154.html">User Profile (XEP-0154)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465327424">8</a>].</p>
<h2 id="former">6.
       Former Usages<a class="anchor-link" href="#former"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h2>
  <p class="" style="">Earlier versions of this document described how to include the User Nickname extension in presence stanzas and invitations sent in relation to <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0045.html">Multi-User Chat (XEP-0045)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465249168">16</a>] rooms. Based on deployment experience, that usage is now discouraged, since it is confusing to display multiple nicknames to an end user and inclusion of user-generated nicknames can override or work around local service policies for "nick lockdown" in chatrooms.</p>
  <p class="" style="">Earlier versions also described usage in relation to the <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0130.html">Waiting Lists (XEP-0130)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465243472">17</a>] protocol. Because that protocol is now obsolete, documentation of such usage has been removed from this specification.</p>
<h2 id="security">7.
       Security Considerations<a class="anchor-link" href="#security"><abbr title="Link to this point in the document">¶</abbr></a></h2>
  <p class="" style="">A nickname is a memorable, friendly name asserted by a user. There is no guarantee that any given nickname will be unique even within a particular community (such as an enterprise or university), let alone across the Internet through federation of communities. Clients SHOULD warn users that nicknames asserted by contacts are not unique and that nickname collisions are possible. Clients also MUST NOT depend on nicknames to validate the identity of contacts; instead, nicknames SHOULD be used in conjunction with JIDs (which are globally unique) and user-assigned handles (which are private and unique) as described in <span class="ref" style=""><a href="https://xmpp.org/extensions/xep-0165.html">Best Practices to Prevent JID Mimicking (XEP-0165)</a></span>  [<a href="#nt-idm46248465372272">3</a>] in order to provide a three-pronged approach to identity validation, preferably in combination with X.509 certificates.</p>
